1) Vocabulary & Clarifications

During the lesson, you clarified several cultural and linguistic terms:
Roscón de Reyes
English: King's Cake
A round sweet bread eaten for Epiphany.
Nata
English: Cream
Usually refers to whipped cream filling in a cake.
Pantalla
English: Screen
Refers to a computer or device screen.
Caber
English: To fit
Used to describe capacity (e.g., “In my car five people fit”).
Valer
English: To cost / To be worth
Used to express value or price of items or people.
Siguiente
English: Next / Following
Al día siguiente means “the next day”.
Sendero
English: Path / Trail
A path through the forest (bosque).
Charlar
English: To chat
An informal way to say “to talk” about life.
Alojamiento
English: Accommodation
General term for a place to stay (hotel, etc.).
Sin duda
English: Without a doubt
Used to say “certainly” or “obviously.”
Inolvidable
English: Unforgettable
Used to describe a special or wonderful day.

2) Periphrastic Future (Ir + a + Infinitive)

“I am going to [verb]” for near-future plans.
Formula: conjugated ir + a + infinitive.
Yo voy a cantar.
I am going to sing.
Vamos a ir a la iglesia.
We are going to go to church.
Vas a desayunar tostadas.
You are going to have toast.
Van a jugar al fútbol.
They are going to play soccer.

3) Simple Future (Futuro Simple)

“I will [verb].” For regular verbs, add endings to the infinitive.
Regular endings (all -ar / -er / -ir)
Endings: -é, -ás, -á, -emos, -éis, -án
Irregular verbs (special future stems) — same endings, different stem. Tap ▶ Speak to hear the whole conjugation.
Tener → Tendr-
Meaning: to have
Future: tendré, tendrás, tendrá, tendremos, tendréis, tendrán
Venir → Vendr-
Meaning: to come
Future: vendré, vendrás, vendrá, vendremos, vendréis, vendrán
Poner → Pondr-
Meaning: to put
Future: pondré, pondrás, pondrá, pondremos, pondréis, pondrán
Poder → Podr-
Meaning: to be able to / can
Future: podré, podrás, podrá, podremos, podréis, podrán
Salir → Saldr-
Meaning: to leave / go out
Future: saldré, saldrás, saldrá, saldremos, saldréis, saldrán
Hacer → Har-
Meaning: to do / make
Future: haré, harás, hará, haremos, haréis, harán
Saber → Sabr-
Meaning: to know (a fact)
Future: sabré, sabrás, sabrá, sabremos, sabréis, sabrán
Querer → Querr-
Meaning: to want / to love
Future: querré, querrás, querrá, querremos, querréis, querrán
